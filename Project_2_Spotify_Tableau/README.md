# Spotify Consumer Behavior & Subscription Analysis

**Domain:** Media & Entertainment / Consumer Behavior  
**Tool:** Tableau Public

## 📌 Project Overview
In the highly competitive audio streaming industry, understanding user habits and subscription drivers is critical for revenue growth. This project analyzes a detailed dataset of 521 Spotify users to uncover actionable insights regarding their demographics, listening routines, and willingness to upgrade to premium tiers. 

The final deliverable is a fully interactive Business Intelligence dashboard designed to allow stakeholders to filter consumer behavior by specific age groups and demographics.

## 🛠️ Methodology & Visualizations
The dashboard was engineered using Tableau to transform raw survey data into four interconnected, interactive visualizations:

1. **Demographic Breakdown (Stacked Bar Chart):** Analyzes the user base by Age and Gender to identify the core audience.
2. **The Revenue Engine (Horizontal Bar Chart):** Compares Free vs. Premium users and highlights the specific volume of ad-supported users who have explicitly stated a willingness to upgrade.
3. **Listening Habits (Heatmap):** Correlates the time of day with the user's emotional state (e.g., relaxation, sadness, social gathering) to identify peak engagement windows.
4. **Top Genres (Packed Bubble Chart):** Visualizes the dominance of specific content categories (Melody, Pop, Rap, Classical) within the ecosystem.

## 💡 Key Business Insights

Based on the visual analysis, several clear trends emerged that can directly impact marketing and product strategy:

* **Prime Conversion Target:** The core user base is overwhelmingly aged 20-35. More importantly, a significant portion of users currently on the "Free (ad-supported)" tier indicated a willingness to pay for a premium subscription, representing immediate, untapped revenue potential if targeted with the right promotional offers.
* **The "Nighttime Relaxation" Window:** The heatmap clearly demonstrates that the highest concentration of app usage occurs at "Night" with the specific intent of "Relaxation and stress relief." Marketing campaigns pushing sleep timers, lo-fi playlists, or acoustic tracks should be heavily concentrated during evening hours.
* **Content Dominance:** "Melody," "Pop," and "Rap" represent the most significant clusters of user interest. Licensing budgets and algorithm recommendations should prioritize these genres for the 20-35 demographic to maximize retention.
